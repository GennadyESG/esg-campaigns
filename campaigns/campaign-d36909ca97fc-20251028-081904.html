EU proposes EUDR relief: micro and small firms get six-month delay, simplified duties

October 23, 2025

The European Commission proposed adjustments to the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), including a full six‑month delay for micro and small companies, pushing their compliance date to December 30, 2026. The plan narrows due‑diligence statement requirements to “upstream” operators and introduces other simplifications intended to reduce administrative burden without weakening environmental goals. Larger and medium companies would still face the December 30, 2025 start, with a transitional grace period to ease enforcement.

Why it matters for SMEs: Exporters of coffee, cocoa, timber, rubber, palm oil and related products can adjust timelines, update traceability systems, and budget for compliance with clearer, lighter requirements.

Hong Kong launches vetted cybersecurity marketplace to strengthen SME governance and resilience

October 23, 2025

The Digital Policy Office and HKCERT unveiled the “Cybersecurity Service Providers Connect Programme,” a one‑stop, vetted platform linking SMEs with qualified security providers across four service pillars. The initiative includes rigorous provider screening, user feedback mechanisms, and a growing roster of 18 approved firms to make it faster and safer for small businesses to procure solutions. Organisers said the programme will expand into cloud and AI security and add certification pathways to raise service quality.

Why it matters for SMEs: Cybersecurity is a core part of ESG governance, and this programme reduces the cost and risk of choosing credible vendors to meet customer and regulator expectations.
