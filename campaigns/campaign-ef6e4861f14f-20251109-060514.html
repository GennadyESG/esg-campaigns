ASEAN issues simplified ESG disclosure guide Version 2 for supply-chain SMEs

November 6, 2025

The ASEAN Capital Markets Forum released Version 2 of the ASEAN Simplified ESG Disclosure Guide (ASEDG) after a five‑month public consultation. The guide streamlines global and local frameworks into practical disclosures tailored for SMEs operating in supply chains. It helps small businesses answer sustainability data requests from customers and lenders with less time and cost.

Why it matters for SMEs: You can use the free guide now to prioritise what to disclose and meet buyer and bank requirements more easily.

Malaysia launches ESG disclosure guide to help batik micro and small producers

November 7, 2025

Capital Markets Malaysia, an affiliate of the Securities Commission, unveiled the Sustainable Batik Disclosure Guide to support artisans, entrepreneurs and producers. The practical tool helps small batik businesses disclose ESG practices in a simple, structured way. It aims to boost market access and recognition for a heritage MSME sector while meeting growing buyer expectations.

Why it matters for SMEs: Batik MSMEs can follow a ready template to answer ESG questionnaires from buyers and retailers and win more orders.

Read more at Securities Commission Malaysia →

UK county expands SME energy support with grants, loans and free assessments

November 7, 2025

Staffordshire County Council highlighted a small business that halved electricity bills after BEAS‑backed solar upgrades. The Business Energy Advice Service offers free energy assessments, 50% match‑funded grants up to £100,000, and interest‑free loans for eligible SMEs. The council also announced an extra £1 million in BEAS funding and urged local firms to apply now.

Why it matters for SMEs: This is immediate help to cut energy costs and emissions, with funded advice plus finance to implement projects.

Read more at Staffordshire County Council →

Penang rolls out ESG training to help SMEs measure emissions and report

November 5, 2025

Penang Green Council, with EY and support from InvestPenang, ran ESG Enhanced Capacity Building sessions for local industries. Modules focus on sustainability reporting and Scope 1 and 2 CO₂ calculations, with a focus on E&E sector SMEs. The training is practical and HRDF‑claimable, helping firms build readiness for supply‑chain expectations.

Why it matters for SMEs: You can quickly upskill teams to produce basic carbon numbers and reports that larger customers now request.
