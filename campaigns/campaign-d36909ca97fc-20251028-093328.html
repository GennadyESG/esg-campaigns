Global taskforce unveils plan to close $4 trillion SME sustainability finance gap

October 14, 2025

A new SME-focused subgroup of the Impact Disclosure Taskforce launched a whitepaper at Climate Week NYC outlining ways to unlock impact and climate finance for small and medium-sized enterprises. The roadmap calls for redesigned financial systems, AI-enabled disclosure tools, and regional “Impact Innovation Labs” to channel capital to SMEs. It highlights that less than 10% of climate finance reaches SMEs despite them representing over 90% of businesses worldwide.

Why it matters for SMEs: Expect new financing pilots, tools, and partnerships you can join to access cheaper sustainability-linked capital.

UK opens £15.5m fund to help SME manufacturers boost resource efficiency

October 22, 2025

Innovate UK launched a competition offering up to £15.5 million across two strands to improve resource and energy efficiency in SME manufacturing. The scheme funds feasibility and industrial research projects that deploy industrial digital technologies to cut energy use and materials waste. Applications close December 10, 2025, with awards supporting projects starting from April 2026.

Why it matters for SMEs: Eligible manufacturers can secure grant funding to lower energy bills and hit environmental targets without heavy upfront costs.

EU delays deforestation rule rollout, easing near‑term supplier compliance burden

October 22, 2025

The EU moved to ease compliance and delay the rollout of its deforestation regulation, giving companies more time to meet traceability and due‑diligence obligations. The adjustment responds to readiness concerns across supply chains handling commodities like coffee, cocoa, palm oil, rubber, soy and timber. Further guidance and phased implementation are expected to reduce immediate administrative pressure while keeping the policy direction intact.

Why it matters for SMEs: Smaller suppliers get extra time to set up traceability systems and update buyer documentation to remain in EU‑bound value chains.

South Australia offers grants up to $75,000 for SME energy upgrades

October 31, 2025

The Powering Business Grants SA program is open to South Australian SMEs and not‑for‑profits to co‑fund energy efficiency projects and solar‑plus‑battery systems. Grants range from $2,500 to $75,000, with separate streams for on‑site renewables and for efficiency upgrades that reduce operating energy use. This round closes on October 31, 2025, with eligibility conditions including active ABN, GST registration, and minimum turnover requirements.

Why it matters for SMEs: This is a fast way to cut power bills and emissions while improving ESG performance with government co‑funding.
